Congratulations to the 2022 Award winners

All winners of the CTBUH 2022 Awards can be viewed here. Overall winners, Award of Excellence winners, and Audience Vote winners in all categories presented their projects at the CTBUH 2022 International Conference, held in-person in Chicago on 9–12 November 2022. Submissions for the 2023 Awards Program will be accepted beginning early 2023.

The CTBUH Awards program honors projects and individuals that have made extraordinary contributions to the advancement of tall buildings and the urban environment, and that achieve sustainability at the highest and broadest level. Awards have been given in over twenty categories including Best Tall Building, Urban Habitat, Innovation, Renovation, Interior Design, Construction, and Engineering.

The CTBUH Awards are an independent review of new projects, judged by a prestigious panel of industry experts. The Awards aspire to provide a more comprehensive and sophisticated view of these important structures, while advocating for improvements in every aspect of performance, including those that have the greatest positive impact on the individuals who use these buildings and the cities they inhabit.

Submissions are also considered for inclusion in a gorgeous coffee table book that highlights the best innovations and projects. See the project category criteria below.

CTBUH Award Categories

The Best Tall Non-Building Award recognizes tall structures that, while not necessarily serving the same purposes of a building, contribute to and positively impact their context. The project must exhibit a quality of “tallness” as defined by the CTBUH Tall Building Criteria. In order to qualify for this award, at least 50% of the structure should be non-occupiable and used for an alternative purpose.

Eligible projects must have be complete to CTBUH standards. Submissions should, at a minimum, contain fully annotated drawings, descriptions of the building’s non-occupiable function(s), and photographs of both the structure and its greater context from multiple perspectives. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle requires that the project must have completed between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2022.

The Urban Habitat Award recognizes those projects that make the most significant impacts on the public realm. Submissions are judged on the merits of the contributions they make to their surrounding environment and should demonstrate sustainability across social, economic, and environmental dimensions. Award-winning projects must be designed with consideration of environmental and cultural contexts and exhibit processes and/or innovations that have enhanced the profession, cities, and the lives of occupants.

An eligible site or master plan must be fully realized at the time of submission. A partially completed multi-phase plan may be considered, but must be significantly progressed to show its urbanistic intent. Submissions should, at a minimum, include full documentation of the “urban habitat” aspect of the project, including but not limited to approved development applications, site plans, photographs, and annotated drawings of buildings on site. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes projects completed between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021.

The Construction Award recognizes projects that have advanced construction technologies or geotechnical engineering. Award-winning projects must display superior quality and complexity over others and include transferable or scalable components or methods. Submissions will be judged based on their potential to positively influence tall building construction practices above and below grade.

Eligible projects must, at a minimum, be under construction and significantly progressed towards completion at the time of submission. Submissions should, at a minimum, include a description of the specific elements of the construction process that contribute positively to the industry, photographs of the site’s progression, and ideally, renderings and/or videos illustrating the techniques in action. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes projects that completed between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021, or are still in an active state of construction and are significantly progressing towards completion.

The Repositioning Award distinguishes the achievements of substantially renovated, retrofitted, and/or reused tall buildings whose post-occupancy changes have extended its possibilities and lifespan. The repositioning strategy must be grounded in a framework bounded by cost efficiency, low environmental impact, positive inhabitant impact, economic vitality, and a dynamic relevance to its context. Award-winning submissions will exceed these standards in taking innovative approaches to either structural or programmatic changes made to the building that are replicable in other projects and advance the available suite of technologies available for building lifespan extension.

Submissions must, at a minimum, provide detailed documentation of repositioning strategy, methodology, and execution; annotated drawings of the building pre- and post-intervention, when available; photographs of pre- and post-intervention, when available. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes projects that completed a substantial renovation between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021.

The Systems Award recognizes projects whose building systems, taken individually or collectively, that contribute replicable, innovative methods to the tall building industry and enhance the livelihood of occupants. Systems may include, but are not limited to, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, vertical transportation, sustainability, energy generation, indoor air quality, and life safety. To be considered, the system must demonstrably advance innovations in systems engineering in an efficient, cost-effective, and sustainable fashion.

This category is inclusive of the former MEP Engineering, Façade Engineering, Life Safety, and Fire & Risk Engineering awards. Submissions must, at a minimum, include technical images and drawings of the system or innovation in question, annotated drawings of the building in which it’s operating, and other relevant information. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes projects that completed between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021, or are still in an active state of construction and are significantly progressing towards completion.

The Space Within Award recognizes projects that facilitate and elevate an exceptional user experience in working, living, and shared spaces. Award-winning projects will integrate both building performance and design elements into supporting the well-being of occupants, including but not limited to MEP, vertical transportation, and smart technology. Although not reserved for interiors only, this award solely focuses on performance and how building users are supported by spatial configuration, orientation of spaces, and the synergy of building systems.

An eligible project must be fully complete to CTBUH standards at the time of submission. Submissions should, at a minimum, include a summary description of the above, including but not limited to photographs of interior spaces for each function of the building, typical floor plans, and annotated building drawings. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes all projects completed between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021.

The Innovation Award recognizes recent innovations that have demonstrated—either through testing, research, or application—their suitability for tall building implementation. Award-winning projects can be either individual building components or a fully proposed project, up to and including master planned sites, given they employ multiple innovative design approaches or systems.

Submissions should, at a minimum, submit a summary description of how the innovative concept would advance the profession’s modus operandi along with visualizations and/or testing, research, and application documentation. The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes designs that were either completed concepts or applied to a building that is actively under construction, between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021.

The Equity, Diversity & Inclusion Award is conferred to an individual, firm, policy, or project that exemplifies the common diversity-centric goals of the industry and which substantially and intentionally addresses historic inequities in the profession. Award winners will have adopted policy, hiring practices, opportunities, or programmatic building designs that are directly purposed to broaden the inclusion of the industry to groups and classes of people who have previously been disadvantaged as a result of deliberate policy choices. The most exemplary nominees will be able to expound upon the tangible outcomes of their work and encourage the same in their colleagues.

The eligibility window for this award in the 2023 cycle includes any eligible party or project that was formally published or completed between January 1, 2019, and December 31, 2021.

This award is bestowed by the CTBUH Board of Trustees and is not open for general submission.

This award recognizes an individual who has made extraordinary contributions to the advancement of tall buildings and the urban environment during their professional career. These contributions and leadership are recognized by the professional community and have significant effects, which extend beyond the professional community, to enhance cities and the lives of their inhabitants. The individual’s contributions may be well known or little known by the public and may take any form, such as completed buildings, research, technology, methods, ideas, or industry leadership.

The candidate may be from any area of specialization, including, but not limited to architecture, building systems, construction, academia, urban planning, development, or management. The award emphasizes the unique, multi-disciplinary nature of the Council and is thus set apart from other professional organizations’ awards for single disciplines.

Additional Notes:

  • The personal attributes of the award recipient should be of high professional integrity and the individual’s contributions generally consistent with the values and mission of the Council.
  • The candidate may or may not be a member of the Council.
  • The award is not intended to be awarded posthumously, although it may be so awarded in some cases.
  • Lifetime Achievement Award winners are expected to comply with the following responsibilities:
    • Present their life’s work at the conference.
    • Attend the annual Awards Ceremony & Dinner at the close of the conference to collect the award.

This award is bestowed by the CTBUH Board of Trustees and is not open for general submission.
